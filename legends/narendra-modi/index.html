🇮🇳 Narendra Modi

From a railway platform tea stall to Prime Minister of India.

← All Legends

Born: 17 September 1950, Vadnagar | Known for: PM of India since 2014, Digital India, Yoga Day | Famous words: “Hard work never brings fatigue. It brings satisfaction.”

Early Life

Narendra Damodardas Modi was born on 17 September 1950 in Vadnagar, a small town in Gujarat, the third of six children in a family of modest means. As a boy he helped at his father's tea stall on the Vadnagar railway platform and later ran a tea stall with his brother. At 17, he left home and travelled across India for about two years — the Himalayas, ashrams, the length of the country — searching for purpose before returning to Gujarat.

The Organisation Man

Modi joined the RSS as a young pracharak (full-time worker), living austerely — a bag, a few clothes, constant travel. He earned a reputation as a tireless organiser and later moved to the BJP, where he rose by managing election campaigns and building the party in state after state. He had never held any elected office when, in 2001, he was suddenly made Chief Minister of Gujarat after a devastating earthquake.

Chief Minister to Prime Minister

He led Gujarat for nearly 13 years, longer than anyone before him, focusing his pitch on development, electricity, roads, and industry — the 'Gujarat model'. His tenure also saw the 2002 Gujarat riots, a deeply painful chapter that remains debated in India's public life. In 2014, promising development and change — 'sabka saath, sabka vikas' (together with all, development for all) — he led his party to the first single-party majority in India in 30 years and became Prime Minister. He won again in 2019 with a bigger majority, and a third term in 2024 — a feat matched only by Nehru.

On the World Stage

As PM, Modi became one of the world's most recognised leaders — greeting stadium crowds from Houston to Sydney. His government's flagship efforts include Swachh Bharat (toilets and cleanliness), Jan Dhan bank accounts for the poor, Digital India and UPI payments, direct gas connections for crores of homes, Make in India, and hosting the G20. He popularised International Yoga Day at the UN, adopted after 177 countries co-sponsored India's proposal — a record.

What We Can Learn

  • Where you start does not decide where you finish.
  • Organisation and discipline are superpowers — decades of groundwork preceded the rise.
  • Communication matters: he turned governance into stories people remember.
