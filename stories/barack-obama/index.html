🇺🇸 Barack Obama

From 'skinny kid with a funny name' to first Black President of America.

← All Legends

Born: 4 August 1961, Honolulu | Known for: 44th US President, Yes We Can, Nobel Peace Prize | Famous words: “Change will not come if we wait for some other person or some other time. We are the ones we've been waiting for.”

Early Life

Barack Hussein Obama was born on 4 August 1961 in Hawaii, to a mother from Kansas and a father from Kenya. His father left when he was two; he was raised by his mother and grandparents, spending part of his childhood in Indonesia. A mixed-race boy with an unusual name, he struggled with identity — later admitting he made poor choices as a teen before books and purpose straightened his path.

Choosing Service

After Columbia University, Obama skipped high-paying jobs and moved to Chicago's poor South Side as a community organizer — helping unemployed families fight for job training and better housing, for a modest salary. Then he went to Harvard Law School, where he was elected the first Black president of the prestigious Harvard Law Review. He returned to Chicago to teach law and enter politics — losing a Congressional race badly in 2000 before winning a Senate seat in 2004.

Yes We Can

That same year, a single speech — 'there is not a liberal America and a conservative America; there is the United States of America' — made the unknown senator a national sensation. In 2008, powered by hope, volunteers, and the slogan 'Yes We Can', he was elected the 44th President of the United States — the first African American ever to hold the office, 45 years after Martin Luther King described his dream. He won re-election in 2012 and received the Nobel Peace Prize in 2009.

The Presidency and After

Obama led America out of its worst economic crisis since the 1930s, gave over 20 million Americans health insurance through the Affordable Care Act ('Obamacare'), and ordered the raid that brought down Osama bin Laden. Grey-haired by the end of eight years, he left office with his family's dignity intact — no scandal touched his White House. He remains a global voice for democracy, and his memoirs and speeches are studied as masterpieces of communication.

What We Can Learn

  • Your story's strange beginnings can become your strength.
  • Serve first — his presidency began in Chicago's poorest streets, not in privilege.
  • Words move nations: learn to speak with honesty and hope.
