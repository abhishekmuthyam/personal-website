🎤 Martin Luther King Jr.

The dreamer who marched a nation toward equality.

← All Stories

Born: 15 January 1929, Atlanta | Known for: Civil rights, 'I have a dream' | Famous words: “The time is always right to do what is right.”

Early Life

Martin Luther King Jr. was born on 15 January 1929 in Atlanta, USA. He grew up under laws that kept Black and white Americans apart — separate schools, separate seats, separate lives. He was a gifted student who entered college at 15 and became a pastor like his father. He studied Gandhi's ideas deeply and believed non-violence could work in America too.

The Bus That Started It All

In 1955, a brave woman named Rosa Parks refused to give up her bus seat to a white passenger and was arrested. King, just 26, led the Montgomery Bus Boycott — for 381 days, Black citizens refused to ride the buses, walking miles to work instead. The Supreme Court finally ruled bus segregation illegal. A movement was born, and King became its voice.

I Have a Dream

For the next decade King led marches, faced jail (29 times!), bombs, and beatings — and answered them all with peace. In 1963, before 250,000 people in Washington, he gave the most famous speech of the 20th century: 'I have a dream' — that his children would 'not be judged by the colour of their skin but by the content of their character.' The Civil Rights Act (1964) and Voting Rights Act (1965) followed, ending legal segregation. He won the Nobel Peace Prize at 35 — the youngest ever at that time. In 1968 he was assassinated, but his dream keeps marching.

What We Can Learn

  • One person's courage (Rosa Parks) plus one person's voice (King) can move a nation.
  • Answer hate with dignity — it wins in the end.
  • “Darkness cannot drive out darkness; only light can do that.”
