🍳 How to Eat — Food Preparation Guide
💡 Best method matters! The same food prepared the right way gives more nutrients and better focus. Wrong preparation = less benefit.
🥚
Eggs
Best: Boiled or Poached
✅ Boiled (soft/hard) — most nutrients retained, no extra oil
✅ Poached — no oil, very healthy
✅ Scrambled with minimal butter/ghee
✅ Omelette with veggies — use 1 tsp oil max
❌ Deep fried egg — avoid, too much oil
💡 Eat 2 boiled eggs every morning. Yolk has choline — don't throw it away!
🥜
Groundnuts / Peanuts
Best: Dry Roasted or Boiled
✅ Dry roasted (in pan, no oil) — crispy, tasty, healthy
✅ Boiled with salt — soft, easy to digest, best for gut
✅ Raw (soaked overnight) — maximum nutrients
⚠️ Roasted in small oil — okay occasionally
❌ Deep fried / masala coated — avoid, too much salt + oil
💡 Dry roast in a pan for 5–7 mins on low flame. Cool and eat. Best evening snack!
🪨
Almonds
Best: Soaked Overnight
✅ Soak 5–6 almonds in water overnight → peel skin → eat morning
✅ Raw (without soaking) — also good
⚠️ Roasted (dry) — okay, slightly less nutrients
❌ Salted / flavoured almonds — avoid, too much sodium
❌ Fried almonds — avoid completely
💡 Soaking removes phytic acid — makes nutrients 2x easier to absorb. Just soak at night, eat morning!
🌰
Walnuts
Best: Raw or Soaked
✅ Raw — eat directly as is, 2–3 pieces
✅ Soaked overnight in water — even better for brain
✅ Crushed and added to oats or curd
⚠️ Dry roasted — okay occasionally
❌ Sugar coated / candy walnuts — avoid
💡 Eat 2 walnuts + 5 almonds every morning. Best brain food combo that exists!
🐟
Fish (Rohu / Katla)
Best: Grilled or Light Curry
✅ Grilled with turmeric + pepper + lemon — maximum omega-3
✅ Light curry (Andhra style with tomato + onion) — very healthy
✅ Steamed fish — best nutrients, minimal oil
✅ Fish rasam / soup — great for recovery dinner
❌ Deep fried fish fry (bajji) daily — too much oil, avoid regularly
💡 Eat fish 2–3 times a week. Andhra-style light curry is perfect — turmeric in it reduces inflammation!
🍗
Chicken
Best: Grilled or Boiled
✅ Grilled chicken (with spices) — high protein, low fat
✅ Boiled / pressure cooked chicken — very clean protein
✅ Chicken soup / clear soup — great for recovery
✅ Chicken curry (less oil) — good with roti
⚠️ Tandoori chicken — okay occasionally (less oil than fry)
❌ Fried chicken / KFC style — avoid on work days
💡 Boil chicken with ginger + garlic + pepper = best protein soup for brain + muscles!
🥣
Oats
Best: Boiled with Milk or Water
✅ Boiled oats + milk + banana + honey — perfect breakfast
✅ Oats upma (with veggies) — savory and filling
✅ Overnight oats (soak in curd/milk) — no cooking needed!
⚠️ Instant oats packets — okay but less fiber than rolled oats
❌ Oat cookies / baked sugary oat bars — avoid
💡 Use rolled oats (not instant). Cook in milk for 5 mins. Add banana + 1 tsp honey. Ready in 10 mins!
🫘
Sprouts (Moong / Chana)
Best: Raw Salad or Lightly Steamed
✅ Raw sprouted moong + lemon + salt + onion — best salad
✅ Lightly steamed (2 mins) — easier to digest
✅ Sprouted moong chilla (pancake) — great breakfast
⚠️ Cooked sprouts curry — okay, slightly less nutrients
❌ Deep fried sprout snacks — avoid
💡 Soak moong overnight → tie in cloth → sprouts ready in 24 hrs! Eat fresh with lemon + salt.
🍌
Banana
Best: Eaten Directly / Fresh
✅ Eat directly as a snack — simplest and best
✅ Sliced into oats or curd — great combo
✅ Banana + peanut butter — power snack before study
✅ Banana smoothie with milk — quick energy drink
❌ Banana chips (fried) — avoid, too much oil
❌ Banana with cold milk at night — can cause cold/cough
💡 Eat a ripe (yellow) banana, not overripe (black). Best eaten morning or before workout/study!
🫘
Dal (Toor / Moong / Masoor)
Best: Pressure Cooked + Light Tadka
✅ Pressure cooked dal + light tadka (mustard + curry leaves + ghee)
✅ Dal soup (thin) — great for digestion
✅ Moong dal — easiest to digest, best for daily use
✅ Toor dal (Andhra style sambar) — full of veggies = complete meal
⚠️ Dal makhani (with cream) — heavy, limit to weekends
💡 Add a small spoon of ghee to dal — ghee helps absorb fat-soluble vitamins. Don't skip it!
🍠
Sweet Potato
Best: Boiled or Roasted
✅ Boiled with skin on — max nutrients, easy to eat
✅ Roasted in oven / air fryer — crispy and delicious
✅ Boiled + mashed with pepper + salt — simple side dish
⚠️ Sweet potato curry — okay, slightly heavy
❌ Sweet potato fries (deep fried) — avoid, defeats the purpose
💡 Boil whole sweet potato for 15 mins. Peel and eat with a pinch of salt. Best dinner side dish!
🥛
Curd / Dahi
Best: Fresh Homemade at Room Temp
✅ Fresh homemade curd — best probiotics for gut + brain
✅ Curd rice (with tempering) — great lunch option
✅ Raita (curd + cucumber/onion) — cooling and healthy
✅ Buttermilk / Chaas (curd + water + jeera) — best summer drink
❌ Cold curd directly from fridge at night — can cause cold
❌ Flavoured / sweetened curd (store bought) — too much sugar
💡 Always eat curd at lunch, not dinner. At night it can slow digestion. Room temperature is best!
🍵
Green Tea
Best: Brewed Fresh, No Sugar
✅ Brew 1 tea bag in hot (not boiling) water for 2–3 mins
✅ Drink plain or with lemon — best for focus
✅ With a pinch of ginger / tulsi — even better immunity
⚠️ With very little honey — acceptable
❌ With milk — milk blocks the antioxidants in green tea
❌ Boiling green tea — destroys nutrients
❌ More than 2 cups/day — causes acidity
💡 Heat water to 80°C (not boiling). Steep 2–3 mins. Drink before study for calm, clear focus!
🍫
Dark Chocolate
Best: 70%+ cocoa, 2–3 pieces only
✅ Eat 2–3 small pieces directly — let it melt on tongue slowly
✅ With green tea in evening — perfect brain booster combo
✅ Brands: Amul Dark (72%), Lindt 70%+ — easily available
⚠️ More than 3–4 pieces — okay occasionally, not daily
❌ Milk chocolate / white chocolate — not the same, avoid
❌ Chocolate biscuits / bars — too much sugar, not real chocolate
💡 Eat it slowly — don't rush! Slow eating releases more flavour and the brain benefit lasts longer.
🥛
Turmeric Milk (Haldi Doodh)
Best: Warm, Homemade, Before Bed
✅ Heat 1 cup milk → add ½ tsp turmeric + pinch of pepper → stir well
✅ Add a pinch of cinnamon + small piece of jaggery for taste
✅ Drink warm (not hot) 30 mins before sleeping
⚠️ With small amount of sugar — okay if needed
❌ Cold turmeric milk — loses the benefit
❌ Store-bought turmeric milk powder — usually has excess sugar
💡 Black pepper in it is KEY — it increases turmeric absorption by 2000%! Never skip the pepper.
🧀
Paneer
Best: Grilled or Lightly Cooked
✅ Grilled paneer with spices — high protein, low fat
✅ Paneer bhurji (scrambled) — quick, tasty, healthy
✅ Paneer + veggie curry (light gravy) — great lunch
✅ Raw paneer cubes (fresh) + salt + pepper — healthy snack
⚠️ Paneer butter masala — heavy gravy, limit to once a week
❌ Deep fried paneer pakora — avoid regularly
💡 Buy fresh paneer from local dairy or make at home. Store-bought packaged paneer has preservatives.
🟤
Roasted Chana (Bengal Gram)
Best: Dry Roasted, Eaten Directly
✅ Buy ready dry roasted chana from shop — eat directly as snack
✅ With jaggery piece — great energy combo
✅ Mixed with onion + tomato + lemon — quick chana chaat
✅ Powdered with jaggery = Sattu — mix in water, amazing drink!
⚠️ Masala roasted chana — okay occasionally (more salt)
❌ Fried chana / chana in oil — avoid
💡 Hyderabad's best cheap snack! 1 handful (30g) = 10g protein. Better than most expensive protein bars!
